Child protection · Venezuela Earthquake 2026

Protect the children

In emergencies, children are the most vulnerable. This guide explains how to act if you find an unaccompanied minor, which organizations to contact, and how to prevent them from falling into the wrong hands.

⚠️ Lesson from the Vargas tragedy (1999): The chaos of the first days caused dozens of children to be transferred without institutional control. Families searched for them for decades. Let's not repeat that mistake. Source: "Tras el eco de un río de voces" (UCAB).
🧒

If you find an unaccompanied child

The main goal: safe and fast family reunification

1
Stay with them and find a safe place
Don't leave them alone. Move away from unstable structures, fires or electrical cables. First protect life, then act.
2
Identify and register their information
Check for any ID on their clothing or body. If they can communicate, note: name and age, parents' or relatives' names, home address or references, school name. Write it on an improvised label or bracelet and place it visibly on their clothing.
3
Document with photos
Take a clear photo of the child alone to record their clothing and features. This will help their parents find them.
4
If injured — take them to medical care
Take them to the nearest health center or contact health personnel. Keep all collected data.
5
Notify competent authorities
Go immediately to the Children's Protection Council (CPNNA) of the municipality, Fire Department, Civil Protection or UNICEF personnel in the area. You can also contact CECODAP for guidance.
6
Document any transfer
If a official or third party takes the child, take a photo of them with the child and ask for their name, organization and credential number.
Legal obligation: According to article 397-A of the LOPNNA, if you are aware of a child alone or separated from their parents, you have a legal duty to act and notify the corresponding CPNNA.
⛔

Who NOT to give a child to

On social media people are commenting: "Where can I get one of these children to take care of them?" While some intentions may be good, good intentions without caution are an enormous danger. Adults posing as false officials to take children have been reported.
  • People who are not direct family or verified official authorities
  • People who insist strangely that you give them the child "to take care of them"
  • People who refuse to have the child reviewed first by authorities
  • People who avoid leaving a signed record, photo or document of who they are
  • Neighbors, acquaintances or third parties without official verification
Golden rule: Handover should only happen to parents, legal representatives, or through measures ordered by the Children's Protection Council (CPNNA). To hand a child to an adult, it is mandatory to present filiation documents verified by a protection counselor.

Supplies to protect children in an emergency

🆔
Identification and location
Identification bracelets — help recognize the child and link them to their family · Brightly colored clothing — makes them easier to spot visually · GPS bracelets — if available · Whistles — for quick location · Paper and markers — for transfer records
📋
If a child is being transferred — everything must be recorded
Child's name · Responsible adult · Contact phone · Place of origin · Place of transfer · Time of transfer · Who authorizes it. Nothing just verbal. Everything must be written down.
🦺
For volunteers working with children
Identified vests · Masks · Gloves · Control lists · Adhesive tape · Whistles · Markers
📵
Basic rules on social media
Do not share photos of children without authorization · Do not publish sensitive locations · Do not spread rumors · Prevention is not an exaggeration, it is responsibility.
